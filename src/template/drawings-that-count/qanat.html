As the party explore deeper into the Earth’s cavernous interior, their instruments and their magnetic and thermal orientation strategies fail: North appears everywhere, the centre of the Earth is mysteriously cooler. Hopelessly lost, and dying of thirst, they strike through a rockface in an attempt to reach an underground river. Only then do they realise that the water gushing through at their feet, like all liquids, knows the way down. Namba’s proposal to restore the eleventh-century Arab underground water channels (qanats) of Palermo as a space of public misnavigation engineers the collapse of orientation in order to explore the pleasures of getting lost and the expansion of psychic space this brings. {% endblock %} {% block nextProjectHref %}"verne.html"{% endblock %} {% block prevProjectHref %}"neutrality.html"{% endblock %} {% block subProjectImgName %}qanat{% endblock %}
